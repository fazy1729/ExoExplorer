THEORY OF RELATIVITY

Einstein's revolutionary framework that transformed our understanding of space, time, and gravity.

Space-Time Continuum

The universe isn't just space with time passing through it - space and time are interwoven into a single continuum that can warp and bend under the influence of gravity.

"Matter tells spacetime how to curve, and spacetime tells matter how to move."

— John Archibald Wheeler

Gravitational Waves

Ripples in the fabric of spacetime caused by the most violent cosmic events.

Time Dilation

Time passes slower in stronger gravitational fields and at higher velocities relative to other observers.

Length Contraction

Objects moving at relativistic speeds appear shorter in the direction of motion to stationary observers.

Mass-Energy

The famous E=mc² equation shows that mass can be converted to energy and vice versa.

PLANETARY SYSTEMS

Worlds beyond our own, each with unique characteristics and mysteries waiting to be uncovered.

Gas Giants

Massive planets composed mainly of hydrogen and helium with no solid surface.

Terrestrial Worlds

Rocky planets with solid surfaces, similar in composition to Earth.

Ice Giants

Planets composed mainly of elements heavier than hydrogen and helium.

Exoplanets

Planets orbiting stars beyond our solar system with diverse characteristics.

STELLAR PHENOMENA

The celestial powerhouses that shape galaxies and forge the elements of life.

Stellar Lifecycle

From protostars to supernovae, the dramatic lives of stars across the cosmos.

Nuclear Fusion

Stars shine by converting hydrogen into helium in their cores through nuclear fusion, releasing enormous amounts of energy in the process. This process also creates heavier elements that are scattered across the cosmos when stars die.

"We are all made of star-stuff."

— Carl Sagan

Supernovae

The spectacular explosions of massive stars that outshine entire galaxies briefly.

Neutron Stars

Incredibly dense stellar remnants where matter is compressed to nuclear density.

Black Holes

Regions of spacetime where gravity is so strong that nothing can escape, not even light.
